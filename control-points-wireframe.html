Human Authorization Plane (HAP)

Human Authorization Infrastructure

Overview

HAP converts authorization from a persistent credential model into a real-time event model.

It verifies that a specific human intentionally approved a specific action at that moment.

HAP produces time-bound authorization artifacts — not reusable credentials.

Any system requiring high-assurance execution gating must resolve this boundary.

Purpose

To enforce real-time, human-performed authorization as a system primitive.

Core Capabilities

  • Live system-initiated authorization challenge
  • Device-bound human verification
  • Non-reusable authorization artifact
  • Time-limited approval events
  • Architectural separation from execution logic

HAP creates a deterministic authorization boundary prior to execution.

The Problem

Most systems rely on reusable credentials, long-lived permissions, or stored identity proofs.

  • Passwords persist long after intent is gone.
  • Credentials can be shared, stolen, or replayed.
  • Biometrics alone can be recorded or synthesized.

As AI, automation, and delegation scale, these mechanisms no longer answer the question that actually matters: Who is authorizing this action right now?

Identity systems authenticate who someone is. They do not verify who is acting in the moment.

What HAP Is

The Human Authorization Plane (HAP) is a standalone system designed to verify real-time, intentional authorization by the correct human at the moment an action is requested.

  • A real human is present
  • The correct authorized human is present
  • The human is physically in control of the device
  • The authorization is intentional
  • The authorization is occurring now

HAP produces time-limited authorization events, not reusable credentials or standing permissions.

Authorization becomes an event, not a credential.
